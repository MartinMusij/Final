Food! Your one stop shop to happiness

Beef is the primary Argentinian dish. Their version of barbeque is called asado (beef grilled on an open fire pit) and it is a must when gathering with friends and family. The famous parillada mixta (a mix of cuts in an asado) might consist of kindeys, sausage, sweetbread, and small intenstine. Bread and salad are the perfect side dishes. The italian food and traditional pasta are also quite common. Not to put aside pizza, a very appreciated and mastered dish. When it comes to drinks, you can be confident to be able to find some of the best and most afordable wines you can find.However, mate is the most traditional drink. It's similar to tea but it's more of a way of socializing than a plain beverage. The most common way to prepare it is to fill 2/3 of a gourd (also called mate) with the leaves of the yerba plant (available in every market), put a silver straw on it (bombilla) and then pour hot water. When the gourd is filled with water, the leaves expand and fill the mate. Some people also prefer to add some sugar. Argentina is also home to some excellent desserts. Ice cream or helado is particularly good, especially from shops that make their own. And you can also enjoy an alfajor, a traditional and very popular sweet layered pastry.
